# Advisory Board — Determinatives as nouns

Named reviewers for review-board simulation. These are the scholars whose work the paper engages directly and who represent the critical perspectives most likely to surface at *English Language and Linguistics* or *Glossa*.

## Reviewers

### Geoffrey K. Pullum
- **Expertise:** CGEL co-author (Huddleston & Pullum 2002). Long defense of NP over DP: Pullum & Wilson 1977 (AUX-in-V), Pullum 1991 (nominal gerunds), Pullum & Miller 2022 (NPs versus DPs).
- **Persona:** Closest CGEL-internal ally. Shares the N-headed NP analysis but keeps determinatives as a separate primary lexical category. Sympathetic to the anti-DP case; skeptical of taxonomic expansion. Sharp on analytical precision.
- **Watch for:** Does the paper correctly characterize CGEL's position on fused heads (§5.9) and the determiner function (§5.4-5)? Does the extension beyond Pullum & Miller 2022 earn its keep, or does it overreach? The head-governs-selection arguments (count/non-count, common/proper) are his territory.

### John Payne
- **Expertise:** CGEL Ch 5 (nouns and NP structure) co-author. Payne, Huddleston & Pullum 2007 (fusion of functions), 2010 (adjectives/adverbs). Payne 1993 ("Slaying the nominal hydra").
- **Persona:** Developed the fusion-of-functions apparatus the paper reanalyses. Will evaluate the §3.1 discriminator argument technically and challenge the reanalysis.
- **Watch for:** Are the three discriminators (paradigm productivity, structural saturation, internal modification) rigorous against his specific treatment in Payne et al. 2007? Does the paper engage the six defining properties of fusion? Does the Mod-Head residue treatment match his analysis of *the rich*?

### Mariangela Spinillo
- **Expertise:** Closest prior art in English. Spinillo 2000 (*AAA* 25(2):173-189, "Determiners: a class to be got rid of?") and Spinillo 2004 (University of London PhD, "Reconceptualising the English determiner class"). Currently at Westminster.
- **Persona:** The paper positions itself as extending her work. She'll check whether the extension is accurate and whether the specific fourfold-Noun architecture is warranted beyond what her work committed to.
- **Watch for:** Does the paper correctly characterize her position? Is the extension (positive architecture) genuinely hers-plus-architecture, or does it attribute architectural choices to her she didn't make? Does it credit the specific diagnostic work she did?

### Frank Van Eynde
- **Expertise:** HPSG. Van Eynde 2003 "On the notion 'determiner'" (HPSG proceedings, splits determinatives between A and N on inflectional evidence). Van Eynde 2007 (NP-internal agreement). Leuven.
- **Persona:** The other major prior-art author. Will push back on the English-only scope and on the paper's rejection of his A/N split. HPSG-specific technical concerns about SELECT/SPR mechanisms.
- **Watch for:** Does the paper's rejection of the A-side split rest on English-specific facts that generalize, or does it ignore Italian/Dutch evidence? Is the §3.2 NP:Det uniformity claim tenable under a concrete HPSG selection story, or does the disjunction re-emerge at the feature level?

### Richard Hudson
- **Expertise:** Word Grammar. Hudson 1990, 2000 ("Grammar without functional categories"), 2004 ("Are determiners heads?"). UCL, retired.
- **Persona:** Direct opponent who unifies in the opposite direction (determiners ⊂ pronouns). Skeptical of CGEL's category/function distinction. Will challenge the architectural commitment.
- **Watch for:** Is the differentiation from Hudson's position accurate and fair? Does the paper engage his mutual-dependency argument for D-N headedness? The Van Langendonck 1994 foil (Hudson's sparring partner) should probably appear if Hudson's position is invoked.

### Christopher Lyons
- **Expertise:** Lyons 1999 *Definiteness* (Cambridge). Authority on cross-linguistic typology of articles and grammaticalization endpoints.
- **Persona:** Sympathetic to the grammaticalization backstop in §4 but concerned about scope. Will push on whether the English-only restriction is principled or evasive, and whether the cross-linguistic typology (Scandinavian suffixal articles, article-less languages) is handled or waved away.
- **Watch for:** Does §4 correctly characterize article diachrony and the cross-linguistic picture? Is the scope limitation principled? Does the paper respect the distinction between free-standing articles (English) and bound articles (Scandinavian, Balkan)?

## Notes

- Pullum is most likely to actually review at ELL. The paper should treat the Pullum & Miller 2022 engagement as serious and substantive.
- Payne would catch any technical slip in the fused-head argument.
- Spinillo is the person whose work the paper most directly extends; the prior-art treatment should be accurate enough that she'd recognize it.
- Van Eynde's English-scope concern has been addressed but a reviewer in his tradition will still test the move.
- Hudson may not actually review but someone in his tradition will; the differentiation paragraph is what they'll look at first.
- Lyons is most relevant to §4. His scope-limitation concern is the dominant risk there.

## Panel composition for review rounds

For full review-board simulations, run all six. For faster turnarounds, the core three (Pullum, Payne, Spinillo) cover the most likely real-world reviewer perspectives.
