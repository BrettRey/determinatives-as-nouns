# Prior-art search results (Phase 0a)

**Date:** 2026-04-20

## Bottom line

**Revised after second pass (2026-04-20, later):** Significant prior art exists in the form of **dissolve-the-Det-category** proposals from Spinillo 2000/2004 and Van Eynde 2003, and from Maekawa 2016 in opposing direction. Brett's fourfold-Noun taxonomy as a specific architectural commitment is still novel, but the "determiner is not a good standalone category" move is not — and the paper must now explicitly differentiate from these three.

The closest third-party proposals are:

| Author | Move | Relation to Brett |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| Abney 1987 (and descendants) | D heads DP | Foil |
| Jackendoff 1977 | Det-headed phrases exist; split into Art and Q | Weaker precursor, compatible |
| Hudson 1990, 2000, 2004 | Determiners are a subset of **pronouns** ("transitive pronouns") | Unification *opposite* direction |
| Postal 1969 | Pronouns are a subset of **determiners** | Unification *opposite* direction |
| Anderson 1997 | Names + pronouns + determiners grouped together, common noun separate | Partial unification, common noun excluded |
| Déchaine & Wiltschko 2002 | Pronouns are not a single category; some DP, some NP, some φP | Decomposition, not unification |
| **Spinillo 2000, 2004** | Determiner class not valid for English; should be dissolved. No explicit taxonomy proposed | **Closest in spirit**; Brett commits to the specific taxonomy Spinillo stops short of |
| **Van Eynde 2003** | Dissolve the Det category; members go to either A (adj) or N (noun/pronoun) based on inflectional morphology | **Closest on mechanism**; but Van Eynde splits between A and N, Brett keeps all in N |
| Maekawa 2016 | Keeps Det distinct from N but handles seminumeral agreement puzzle | Opposing, preserves Det category |
| Herslund 2008 | Definite articles are pronominal heads; indefinite articles are quantifiers/classifiers | Partial nominal move, articles only |
| Bruening 2020 | Head of the nominal is N, not D | Ally on headedness, silent on taxonomy |
| Pullum & Miller 2022 | N heads substantive phrases (classical view) | Ally on headedness, explicit separation of Det category from N |
| Chomsky 2020 | Retracts DP, says "core system is basically nominal" | Gestures toward nominal, determiners as adjuncts |

**Interpretation.** Brett's fourfold taxonomy is novel as a specific architectural commitment. The architecture (supercategory Noun with four subtypes) has not been published. This is the extension of CGEL's pronoun-as-noun move to determinatives. But the **dissolve-the-Det-category move itself has clear precedent in Van Eynde 2003 (HPSG) and Spinillo 2000/2004 (English-specific).** So the paper's novelty lies in (a) the specific fourfold partition, (b) the CGEL compatibility, and (c) the inclusion of all determinatives under N rather than splitting them. The paper must explicitly differentiate from Van Eynde and Spinillo.

**Key new analogy from Van Eynde 2003.** Van Eynde explicitly cites **Pullum & Wilson 1977** "Autonomous syntax and the analysis of auxiliaries" (*Language* 53:741-788) and **Gazdar, Pullum & Sag 1982** as the precedent for dissolving a functional category into substantive categories. These papers dissolved the AUX category into V. That is the exact parallel move Brett is making: dissolve Det into N. In-house, CGEL-compatible, and uses a published Pullum precedent. This is a much stronger analogy than *to*-as-auxiliary.

## Key citations to stage

### Survey and recent NP/DP debate
- **Blümel & Holler 2022** "DP, NP, or neither? Contours of an unresolved debate" *Glossa*. The recent survey. Locates Brett's proposal in the "or neither" strand.
- **Salzmann 2020** "The NP vs. DP debate. Why previous arguments are inconclusive..." *Glossa* 5(1).
- **Bruening 2020** "The head of the nominal is N, not D" *Glossa* 5(1):15. Strong ally.
- **Bruening 2009** "Selectional asymmetries between CP and DP suggest that the DP hypothesis is wrong" *Penn Working Papers*.
- **Bruening, Dinh, Kim 2018** "Selection, idioms, and the structure of nominal phrases with and without classifiers" *Glossa* 3(1):1-46.
- **Osborne & Gerdes 2021** "NPs, not DPs: The NP vs. DP debate in the context of dependency grammar" *Acta Linguistica Academica* 68(3).
- **Georgi 2020** "Agreement, disagreement and the NP vs. DP debate" *Glossa*.

### DP defenders (foils)
- **Abney 1987** *The English Noun Phrase in its Sentential Aspect*. PhD, MIT. (In Mendeley.)
- **Szabolcsi 1983** "The possessor that ran away from home" *Linguistic Review* 3. (Hungarian, DP origin.)
- **Fukui & Speas 1986** "Specifiers and projection" *MIT Working Papers* 8.

- **Larson 2019** "The DP hypothesis and (a)symmetries between DP and CP" *Linguistic Analysis* 42.

### Middle/compromise
- **Jackendoff 1977** *X-bar Syntax*. Article and Quantifier as determinative-heading categories.
- **Radford 1993** "Head-hunting: on the trail of the nominal Janus" (in *Heads in Grammatical Theory*, Corbett/Fraser/McGlashan eds.). NP+DP compromise.
- **Payne 1993** "The headedness of noun phrases: Slaying the nominal hydra" (same volume). NP defence.

### Unification proposals (opposite directions)
- **Postal 1969** "On so-called pronouns in English". Pronouns as determiners. (In Mendeley.)
- **Hudson 1990** *English Word Grammar*.
- **Hudson 2000** "Grammar without functional categories" (in Corbett/Fraser, *The nature and function of syntactic categories*). (In Mendeley.)

- **Van Langendonck & Van de Velde 2016** "Names and Grammar".
- **Gardelle 2019** *Semantic Plurality: English Collective Nouns and Other Ways of Denoting Pluralities of Entities*. Benjamins.
