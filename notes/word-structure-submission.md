# *Word Structure* — submission notes

Target journal as of 2026-04-29 (DECISIONS.md).

## Mechanics

- **Submit by email** to the Editor. Look up current editor on the journal site before sending.
- **Fully anonymized**, including PDF metadata. Self-references in third person (e.g., "Rodriguez (2025) argues" not "I argue in Rodriguez (2025)").
- **Abstract**: ≤ 250 words.
- **Keywords**: 3-5.
- **No word limit** specified for research articles.
- **No specific formatting** required for initial submission. On acceptance, format per the [Generic Style Rules for Linguistics](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.253501).
- **Figures**: high-resolution required before production; provide alt text for each.
- **Timeline**: pre-screening up to 2 weeks; double-blind review ~3 months.

## Scope fit

*Word Structure* aims "to further the understanding of the nature of words, in every sense and in the broadest definition," and explicitly includes syntax alongside morphology. A lexical-category-taxonomy paper is in scope, but the journal is morphology-leaning. Possible adjustment: foreground word-class architecture as a question about the nature of words, not just CGEL-internal taxonomy. The auxiliary-as-verb precedent (Pullum & Wilson 1977) plays well here as a canonical paper about how lexical-category boundaries are drawn.

## Pre-submission checklist

- [ ] Look up current editor email on [journal site](https://www.euppublishing.com/journal/word/submit).
- [ ] Verify abstract ≤ 250 words.
- [ ] Check keyword count (3-5).
- [ ] Convert any first-person self-citations to third person.
- [ ] Strip identifying metadata from the PDF (`exiftool -all=`).
- [ ] Confirm anonymized PDF: no author name, no acknowledgements, no funding statements that identify.
- [ ] Confirm references list does not over-cite Reynolds in a way that de-anonymizes.
